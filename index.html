Some things about me

I am a growing UX Designer based in Baltimore, MD with a background in psychology and oncology research. An innovative professional passionate about creating accessible interfaces to serve the needs of any user.

My background in psychology has given me a familiarity with people and an understanding of the research methods to better understand them and their behavior. On your team, I can bring the following skills:

The theories, rules, experimentation, and data analysis that shape a product:

I am academically trained in psychological research; taking a hypothesis through testing and data analysis to iterations and finally, a conclusion. In UXR, I can use these skills to design research informed products that directly benefit people.

While attractive design can be a powerful tool to attract users, research has the power to retain users by providing a product designed to serve their needs.

Understanding emotions, behaviors, and motivations:

I spent four years studying the methods for identifying the “why” behind human behavior. This is a crucial understanding to build and improve products. This understanding helps me to communicate and emphasize with users. I want users to feel seen both in my treatment of them and in the product I design for them.

If you want to see my work in action, peruse my portfolio. You will see my demonstrate skills in the design process, from user empathizing to prototype testing. There might even be a dash of front-end development there.
